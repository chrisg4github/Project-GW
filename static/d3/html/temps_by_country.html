Yearly Average Temperature(F)

During the past five decades, the earth has been warming at a rather high rate, in scientific terms, of approx .108F. This warming trend is observed in and affects the atmosphere both over the land and over the ocean. Several scientific research sources assert this temperature increase is primarily caused by the increase in the atmospheric concentration of CO2 during the last 200 years.

However, there are difficulties in reading this trend when there are other simultaneous cyclical events occurring in various global regions. Significant among these are Volcanic eruptions and solar events. Both these have been examined and found to be of no cumulative consequence to global warming.

Fossil fuel consumption is widely considered the main cause of the quantifiable increase in CO2 emissions. A dramatic difference can be seen in the CO2 amounts attributed to under developed countries vs those more advanced.
